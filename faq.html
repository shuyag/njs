Can Sheprd transport Special Needs Pupils?

No. Pursuant to the Mass RMV Guide to School Pupil Transport Vehicles (7D) Chapter 90 Sec. 7CC, Sheprd does not maintain the appropriate vehicle signage to transport Special Needs Pupils.

Will Sheprd walk my child to / pick my child up from the door of their origin or destination?

No. Sheprd will drive your child to the destination and ensure his/her safe exit from the vehicle. Likewise, Sheprd will pick your child up from the origin and ensure his/her safe entrance into the vehicle. Much like the School Bus driver, pursuant to 7D regulations, Sheprd drivers cannot leave the vehicle unattended.

What if my child needs to be “signed-in” or “signed-out” from his/her activity?

Unfortunately, because Sheprd drivers are unable to leave their vehicle unattended, Sheprd is also unable to “sign-in” or “sign-out” a passenger. Sheprd’s involvement more closely parallels the School Bus driver than a chauffeur.

Are rides available On-Demand or do I have to schedule my rides in advance?

Sheprd rides are not available On-Demand and may be scheduled up to any time up to or before 11:59PM the day prior to travel.

What are Sheprd’s hours of operation?

Sheprd’s current hours of operation run from 3:00PM ET – 7:00PM ET Monday through Friday, with certain exceptions for school holidays and vacations. If you require transportation outside of these hours, we urge you to be patient as we work on expanding. If you have a group of students requiring transport outside of these hours, please contact us directly.

To / from where can my child take Sheprd?

Sheprd currently mandates that your child’s trip origin or destination be a location shared with our kids. Below are some examples of what we mean:

School to Mom’s house? Sure thing.
Grandma’s house to School? No problem.
Practice to Dad’s house? Yep.
Mom’s house to Grandma’s house? This one is a “No can do”.

Will my child be required to wear a seatbelt while in Sheprd?

Yes, the driver will not depart until all children are safely and appropriately buckled. (Mass RMV Guide to School Pupil Transport Vehicles (7D) Chapter 90 Sec. 13A.)

What age range of children are permitted to take Sheprd?

Sheprd’s passengers are limited to between the ages of 9 and 16, primarily for the reason that Sheprd’s vehicles are not equipped with child passenger restraint systems (booster seats). (Mass RMV Guide to School Pupil Transport Vehicles (7D) Chapter 90 Sec. 7AA.)

In what towns is Sheprd currently available?

Sheprd is currently only available in the town of Newton, MA; though we are working diligently on expanding.

Do you offer babysitting services?

No. Sheprd offers exclusively transportation services for kids.

Will I be able to meet the driver at any time before pick-up?

No. Sheprd’s drivers have been through rigorous checks of their professionalism, background, and ability to carry out their responsibilities effectively. you will be able to review the driver’s profile once you’ve scheduled your trip.

Can I ride with my child in Sheprd?

No, Sheprd is exclusively for kids aged 9-16; adults other than the driver are not permitted in Sheprd vehicle during operation.

What if my child misbehaves during a Sheprd trip?

The driver will inform Sheprd of difficult passengers. If your child receives too many negative reviews he or she will be suspended from using Sheprd.

What if my child is late for the Sheprd trip?

We encourage you to be attentive to vehicle arrival notifications to be On-Time for departure. Sheprd will send notifications two minutes before the vehicle is scheduled to arrive, then again when the vehicle does arrive. The driver will wait for the passenger for 3 minutes after arrival before departing. If the passenger fails to appear, you will be charged a $10 cancellation fee and the vehicle will depart.

What if I want to report a comment about my child’s Sheprd trip or driver?

Following your child’s trip, the application will present a ratings chart and a comments box. This is the appropriate place to provide feedback.

What if my child misses his/her Sheprd?

Sheprd operates similarly to the School Bus, if your child misses the school bus they will be required to arrange alternate transportation arrangements.

What if I need to cancel or make a change to my child’s trip?

You have until 11:59PM ET the day prior to your child’s trip to make changes to the trip without consequence. After this time, you will be responsible for the trip’s expense or a $10 cancellation charge.

Sheprd’s vehicles are School Pupil Transport Vehicles. What are School Pupil Transport Vehicles?

M.G.L. c. 90, Sec 7D allows “school pupils” to be transported in vehicles other than school buses. These vehicles are called “school pupil transport vehicles” (or “7D vehicles” after the Section number creating the law). The law restricts them to carrying a maximum of eight (8) passengers.

The individuals whom Sheprd transports must be School Pupils. What is a “School Pupil”?

Massachusetts law defines a “school pupil” as any person enrolled in any school, kindergarten through grade twelve, or enrolled for day care services, or in any program for children with special needs as defined in M.G.L. c. 71, Sec. 1, or in any organized day or summer camp program or any activity supported by said schools.

How will my child know which Sheprd vehicle is his/hers?

Sheprd vehicles are all similarly-styled Land Rover vehicles sporting Sheprd’s logo and a lighted school bus sign so as to be immediately distinguishable from a passenger vehicle. Sheprd vehicles are further numbered to be easily identified. Prior to your child’s trip, the Sheprd application will provide the vehicle’s number and the driver’s name. The driver and passenger will verify mutual identity before the passenger enters the vehicle.

When will I be charged for trip?

Though you are asked to store your bank/credit card information in the Sheprd application, you will not be charged until a ride has been completed, or a ride had a late cancellation.

How much do rides cost?

Sheprd rides start at $17 / one-way trip / passenger

Driver FAQ

What Qualifications do I need to become a driver?

A high standard of ethical and moral conduct
A basic understanding of mobile phone applications like UBER / LYFT
To understand American English conversational voice, to speak and be understood via phone and in person
Applicants must be able to clearly and effectively communicate with children, parents, and staff
A valid Massachusetts 7D driver's license (pre or post-application)
21 years of age or older
3+ years driving experience
2+ years child-care experience
In-state Driver’s License (Massachusetts)
Clean driving record
Passing of Sheprd Secure Background Check (CORI/SORI Inclusive)
Full state & federal screening
Must be able to pass background check and drug tests and physical
Citizenship, residency or work VISA in United States
Pediatric First Aid Certification (pre or post-application)

What Responsibilities do I have as a Driver?

Follow directions from a mobile application to safely drive children to and from a variety of afterschool events
Ensure safety and well-being of children who are being transported at all times, including loading and unloading
Perform pre/post trip vehicle inspections
Maintain a clean vehicle interior
Ensure that no child is left unattended in the vehicle
Monitor the health of each child in the vehicle
Interact with and caring for school age children
Maintain a calm, cheerful and pleasant demeanor at all times
Maintain control over student passengers, utilizing effective communications and student management techniques
Participate in driver training courses
